Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2025-08-25

MealMiles (“we”, “our”, “us”) provides a loyalty and ordering platform for restaurants (“Partners”) and their customers. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and share information when you use our apps, websites, and services.

Information We Collect

  • Account information such as name, email, phone.
  • Order details and loyalty activity.
  • Payment tokens & metadata processed by Stripe. We do not store full card numbers.
  • Device/usage data for performance, security, and fraud prevention.
  • Restaurant content (menus, photos, hours) provided by Partners.

Legal Bases for Processing

We process personal data to perform our contract with you, comply with legal obligations, protect our legitimate interests (such as preventing fraud), and with consent where required.

How We Use Data

  • Provide and improve ordering, loyalty, and customer support.
  • Facilitate payments and payouts via Stripe.
  • Prevent fraud, abuse, and security risks.
  • Comply with applicable laws and enforce our Terms.

Sharing of Information

We share data with service providers (e.g., Stripe, Firebase, hosting) under strict agreements. We may disclose data as required by law, to protect rights and safety, or in connection with business transfers (e.g., merger, acquisition).

Data Retention

We retain information only as long as necessary for the purposes described above, unless a longer retention period is required by law.

Children’s Privacy

Our services are not directed to children under 13 (or the minimum age required in your country). We do not knowingly collect information from children. If we learn we have, we will delete it.

International Transfers

Data may be processed outside your country, including in the United States and Canada, where laws may differ. We take measures to ensure transfers are legally compliant.

Your Choices

You may access, update, or request deletion of certain data by contacting support@getmealmiles.com.

Security

We use reasonable safeguards (encryption, access controls) to protect information. No system is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.
